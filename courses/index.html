UPSC

UNION PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION

UPSC is an examination conducting body which comes under Department of Personal and Training.

It is a body which has been granted its charter in Part XIV of the Indian Constitution. This body is responsible for appointments to and examinations for All India Services and Grade A and Grade B of Central Services.

The Commission directly reports to the President of India and can advise the government through him. The commission has a Constitutional Status and hence operates with utmost autonomy and freedom.

The commission has its Headquarter at Dholpur House, Delhi.

There are different Examinations that UPSC conducts every year which are listed down as follows:
  • Indian Forest Service Examination
  • Engineering Services Examination.
  • Combined Defence Services Examination.
  • National Defence Academy Examination.
  • Naval Academy Examination.
  • Combined Medical Services Examination
  • Indian Economic Service.
  • Indian Statistical Service Examination.
  • Combined Geo Scientist and Geologist Examination.
  • Central Armed Police Forces (Assistant Commandant) Examination.
Civil Services Examination

This examination is conducted nationwide by UPSC for various Civil Services of the Government of India. Which include services like Indian Administrative Services(IAS), Indian Police Services(IPS), Indian Foreign Services(IFS), Indian Revenue Services(IRS) among others..

This Examination is conducted in three phases.
  1. Preliminary Examination- 400 Marks
  2. Mains Examination- 1750 Marks
  3. Personality Test(Interview)- 275 Marks
A) Preliminary Examination- 400 Marks

This is the entry level examination conducted every year in the month of June advertised in the month of Feb-March. The examination pattern is of Multiple Choice Question(MCQ) based.

It is a one day examination divided into 2 papers each of 200 Marks.

  • Paper-1 (General Studies).
  • Paper-2 (Civil Services Aptitude Test(CSAT)).

Paper -2 is only qualifying in nature whose qualifying is 33% of 200. Once, a candidate secures the cut-off their paper-1 i.e General Studies will be checked. The cut-off to qualify for the Mains examination is determined by the score one secures in Paper-1 i.e General Studies.

B) Mains Examination- 1750 Marks

This examination is Subjective in nature conducted in October every year. The candidate has to appear for 9 papers as follow:

# Subject Marks
1 English Language (Qualifying) 300
2 One of the languages listed in 8th schedule of Indian Constitution (Qualifying) 300
3 Essay 250
4 GS-I (Indian heritage and culture, history and geography of the world and society) 250
5 GGS-II (Governance, constitution, Polity, Social Justice and International Relations) 250
6 GS-III (Technology, Economic development, bio-diversity, environment, security and disaster management) 250
7 GS-IV (Ethics, Integrity and Aptitude) 250
8 2 PAPERS on subjects to be selected by the candidate from the list of optional subjects 250 each
Sub-Total 1750
C) Personality Test(Interview)- 275 Marks

The objective of Personality test is to asses the stability of a candidate for a career in public services by a board of competent and unbiased observers.

Final selection of a candidate is depended upon their total score obtained in Mains and Personality Test which determines a candidateâ€™s rank.
